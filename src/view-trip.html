Where you'll roam
👆 Use each day's In the trip / Add switch to include or drop a whole city · tap a single stop to skip just that activity.
Day 1Chicago — land & explorehome baseno driving
  • Millennium Park & Cloud Gate — the mirror “Bean,” the classic first photo.
  • The Art Institute of Chicago — one of the best art museums in the country.
  • Skydeck at Willis Tower — step onto The Ledge, 103 floors up.
  • Chicago Riverwalk + architecture river cruise — the best way to see the skyline.
  • Eat: deep-dish at Lou Malnati’s or Pequod’s, and an Italian beef at Al’s #1.
  • Hidden gem: the 606 elevated trail, or the free Garfield Park Conservatory.
Day 2Springfield, IL capital
  • Abraham Lincoln Presidential Library & Museum — the headline stop, genuinely great.
  • Lincoln Home National Historic Site — walk his actual restored neighborhood.
  • Old State Capitol — where Lincoln gave his “House Divided” speech.
  • Route 66 kitsch on the way: the Gemini Giant, Funks Grove maple “sirup.”
  • Eat: a Springfield “horseshoe” + the Cozy Dog Drive In, birthplace of the corn dog.
  • Hidden gem: Lincoln’s Tomb at Oak Ridge — rub his nose for luck.
Day 3Indianapolis, IN capital
  • Indianapolis Motor Speedway & Museum — take the track tour and “kiss the bricks.”
  • Canal Walk & White River State Park — paddle-boats, bikes, riverside strolling.
  • Soldiers’ & Sailors’ Monument — climb it, then wander the Mass Ave arts district.
  • On the way back: Indiana Dunes National Park — dunes, Lake Michigan, and the Chicago skyline across the water.
  • Eat: a breaded pork tenderloin sandwich and St. Elmo’s fiery shrimp cocktail.
  • Hidden gem: Newfields / Indianapolis Museum of Art and its Art & Nature Park.
OptionalMadison, WI capital
  • Wisconsin State Capitol — climb to the dome overlook; the farmers’ market rings the square on Saturdays.
  • State Street & UW campus — half a mile of shops and buskers down to Lake Mendota.
  • Memorial Union Terrace — sunburst chairs, a beer, and sunset over the lake.
  • Eat: fried cheese curds, a bratwurst, and a Spotted Cow (sold only in Wisconsin).
  • Hidden gem: the National Mustard Museum in nearby Middleton.
OptionalMinneapolis – St. Paul, MN capital
  • Minnesota State Capitol (St. Paul) — a gleaming white-marble dome, free tours.
  • Mall of America — yes, the one with indoor roller coasters.
  • Stone Arch Bridge & St. Anthony Falls — the Mississippi right downtown.
  • Walker Art Center — and the Spoonbridge and Cherry sculpture garden.
  • Eat: a Jucy Lucy (cheese-stuffed burger) and wild-rice soup.
  • Hidden gem: Minnehaha Falls, a 53-ft waterfall inside the city.
OptionalMilwaukee, WI
  • Milwaukee Art Museum — the Calatrava “wings” that unfurl over the lake.
  • Historic Third Ward & the Public Market — a food hall and boutiques.
  • A brewery tour — Lakefront, the old Pabst, or Miller in beer city.
  • Eat: a Friday fish fry and a scoop of frozen custard.
  • Hidden gem: the Bronze Fonz on the Riverwalk.
