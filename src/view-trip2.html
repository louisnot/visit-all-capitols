Day 1Seattle, WAhome baseno driving
  • Pike Place Market & the waterfront — the original, plus a ferry across Puget Sound.
  • Space Needle & Chihuly Garden — the skyline and the glass.
  • Day trip: Mount Rainier or Snoqualmie Falls — the mountain looms over everything.
  • Eat: fresh salmon, oysters, and a pour-over in coffee's home town.
  • Hidden gem: the ferry to Bainbridge at sunset for the skyline view back.
Day 2Olympia & Olympic NP, WA capital
  • Washington State Capitol — the tallest domed capitol in the U.S.
  • Hoh Rain Forest — moss-draped, emerald, straight out of a fantasy film.
  • Hurricane Ridge — alpine meadows and a wall of glaciated peaks.
  • Ruby Beach & the wild coast — sea stacks, driftwood, tide pools.
  • Hidden gem: Lake Crescent's impossibly clear water.
OptionalMount Rainier NP, WA
  • Paradise wildflower meadows — the classic postcard of Rainier.
  • Reflection Lakes — the mountain mirrored, best at dawn.
  • Grove of the Patriarchs — a boardwalk through 1,000-year-old cedars.
  • Hidden gem: the Skyline Trail if the snow's melted out.
OptionalPortland & the Columbia Gorge, OR
  • Multnomah Falls & the Historic Columbia River Highway — waterfall after waterfall.
  • Mount Hood & Timberline Lodge — the peak from The Shining's exterior.
  • Portland — food carts, Powell's Books, Forest Park.
  • Eat: Voodoo Doughnut, food-cart everything, and Oregon pinot.
OptionalSalem & the Willamette Valley, OR capital
  • Oregon State Capitol — the gold Pioneer on top.
  • Silver Falls & the Trail of Ten Falls — walk behind a 177-ft waterfall.
  • Willamette Valley wine country — rolling vineyards and tasting rooms.
OptionalCrater Lake NP, OR
  • Rim Drive — 33 miles around the deepest, bluest lake in America.
  • Wizard Island & Cleetwood Cove — the one trail down to the water.
  • Hidden gem: the Pinnacles — eerie volcanic spires.
OptionalBoise & the Sawtooths, ID capital
  • Boise Greenbelt & downtown — a surprisingly good river city.
  • Stanley & Redfish Lake — the Sawtooth range reflected in alpine water.
  • Craters of the Moon — a black lava landscape astronauts trained on.
OptionalGlacier NP & Helena, MT capital
  • Going-to-the-Sun Road — one of the great drives on Earth.
  • Lake McDonald & Avalanche Lake — turquoise water, red rock beaches.
  • Helena — Montana's capital and Last Chance Gulch.
  • Hidden gem: Many Glacier at golden hour.
OptionalYellowstone & Grand Teton, WY
  • Old Faithful & the geyser basins — the earth breathing.
  • Grand Prismatic Spring — the rainbow hot spring from every poster.
  • Grand Teton & Jenny Lake — the most dramatic skyline in the Rockies.
  • Lamar Valley — bison, wolves, bears — America's Serengeti.
OptionalCheyenne, WY capital
  • Wyoming State Capitol & downtown — frontier and rail history.
  • Curt Gowdy & Vedauwoo — wind-carved granite on the high plains.

Good to know

This is big country — distances between the wild places are long, and the mountain roads are slow and stunning. Set your days at the top and add stops until the difficulty bar tips into amber; that's the honest edge of what's comfortable. Glacier and Yellowstone each deserve their own multi-day slice.
