Day 1Chicago — land & exploreno driving
  • Millennium Park & Cloud Gate — the mirror “Bean,” the classic first photo.
  • The Art Institute of Chicago — one of the best art museums in the country.
  • Skydeck at Willis Tower — step onto The Ledge, 103 floors up.
  • Chicago Riverwalk + architecture river cruise — the best way to see the skyline.
  • Eat: deep-dish at Lou Malnati’s or Pequod’s, and an Italian beef at Al’s #1.
  • Hidden gem: the 606 elevated trail, or the free Garfield Park Conservatory.
Day 2Chicago → Springfield, IL🚗 ~3.5 h · 200 mi · I-55
  • Morning in Chicago: the Museum Campus (Field Museum, Shedd Aquarium) or the free Lincoln Park Zoo before you roll out.
  • Route 66 kitsch on the way: the Gemini Giant in Wilmington, Funks Grove maple “sirup.”
  • Abraham Lincoln Presidential Library & Museum — the headline stop, genuinely great.
  • Lincoln Home National Historic Site — walk his actual restored neighborhood.
  • Old State Capitol — where Lincoln served and gave his “House Divided” speech.
  • Eat: a Springfield “horseshoe” (open-face sandwich drowned in cheese sauce) + the Cozy Dog Drive In, birthplace of the corn dog.
  • Hidden gem: Lincoln’s Tomb at Oak Ridge — rub his nose for luck.
Day 3Springfield → Indianapolis, IN🚗 ~3.5 h · 200 mi
  • Indianapolis Motor Speedway & Museum — take the track tour and “kiss the bricks.”
  • Canal Walk & White River State Park — paddle-boats, bikes, riverside strolling downtown.
  • Soldiers’ & Sailors’ Monument — climb it for the skyline, then wander the Mass Ave arts district.
  • Eat: a breaded pork tenderloin sandwich (Indiana classic) and the world-famous fiery shrimp cocktail at St. Elmo Steak House.
  • Hidden gem: Newfields / Indianapolis Museum of Art and its Art & Nature Park.
Day 4Indianapolis → Chicago — home the scenic way🚗 ~3 h · 180 mi · I-65
  • Indiana Dunes National Park — the perfect last hurrah: hike the dunes, toes in Lake Michigan, and the Chicago skyline floating across the water.
  • Optional: Fair Oaks Farms right off I-65 for a dairy/ice-cream break.
  • Back to Chicago — you’re home by evening.
  • Eat: one last Chicago-style hot dog (no ketchup, they’ll judge you).

Good to know

This loop checks off 2 of your 50 capitals — Springfield and Indianapolis. It’s an easy triangle: no single drive is over ~3.5 hours, and every leg has a worthwhile stop. Book the Lincoln Museum and a Speedway tour ahead on busy weekends.
